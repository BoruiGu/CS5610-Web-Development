Week 9 - Player - Layout

This experiment is based on two previous experiments: Player - Change and Fixed to Bottom
Player controls live in a navbar fixed to bottom of the page.
Note: Prev & Next functionality have not yet implemented. The two buttons are there only for showing the layout.

Progress: {{progress}}ms

Duration: {{duration}}ms

Volume: {{volume}}%

<nav class="navbar navbar-inverse navbar-fixed-bottom">
    <div class="row">
        <div class="col-md-2"></div>
        <div class="col-md-8 playerWrap">
            <div class="btn-Prev">
                <a ng-click="prev()"><span class="glyphicon glyphicon-step-backward"></span></a>
            </div>
            <div class="btn-Play">
                <a ng-click="startPlaying()" ng-hide="playerStarted"><span class="glyphicon glyphicon-play"></span></a>
                <a ng-click="pause()" ng-show="isPlaying"><span class="glyphicon glyphicon-pause"></span></a>
                <a ng-click="resume()" ng-show="isPaused"><span class="glyphicon glyphicon-play"></span></a>
            </div>
            <div class="btn-Next">
                <a ng-click="prev()"><span class="glyphicon glyphicon-step-forward"></span></a>
            </div>
            <div class="bar-progress">
                <input type="range" ng-model="progress" ng-change="changeProgress()" min="0" max="{{duration}}" />
            </div>
            <!--orient="vertical" for firefox-->
            <input ng-show="showVolumeSlider" id="volumeSlider" type="range" orient="vertical" ng-model="volume" ng-change="changeVolume()" min="0" max="100" />
            <div class="btn-volume">                        
                <a ng-click="showVolumeSlider = !showVolumeSlider"><span class="glyphicon glyphicon-volume-up"></span></a>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>
</nav>
